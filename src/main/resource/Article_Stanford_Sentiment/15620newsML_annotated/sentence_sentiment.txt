Unilever detergent adsparks 'racist' charge:Negative
LONDON 1996-08-28ATV commercial for Unilever's flagship Persil detergentshowing a white horse escaping from a group of black horses has beenaccused by viewers of promoting racism, theindustry watchdogsaid on Wednesday:Negative
Britain's Independent Television Commission (ITC), which licenses and regulates Britain's commercial TV, said 32 viewersprotestedthat the image implied that white is superior to black and "therefore carried a racist message":Very negative
But the ITC said it was not banning the commercial as most viewers were unlikely to have found it offensive:Negative
However the watchdog urged careful vetting ofadvertisements using imageswhich could bemisconstruedas "racial metaphors":Neutral
The Persil commercial, part of a Unilever campaign to show the cleaning powers of its newly-relaunched washing powder, also featured an ice skater dressed in white speeding away from his black-garbed companions, and a dalmatian dog shaking itself so that all its black spots flew off:Very negative
Unilever told the ITC it had created visual metaphors to support the soap powder's slogan 'To set clean free':Neutral
Research on the likely acceptability of the commercial had included talks with Afro-Caribbean women, the company added:Negative
The ad was created for Unilever by J Walter Thompson:Neutral
Complaints about another recent ad campaign were also based on the issue of race:Neutral
