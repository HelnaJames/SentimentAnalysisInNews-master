Bass may see Castlemaine licence review - FT
LONDON 1996-08-27British brewer Bass Plc could face a review of a licensing deal to make and distribute the lager brand Castlemaine XXXX under its deal for control of Carlsberg-Tetley, theFinancial Timesreported
Bass struck a complex agreement over the weekend giving it control of Carlsberg-Tetley, which is owned by Allied Domecq and Denmark's Carlsberg
TheFinancial Timessaid in its Tuesday edition that the licence to produce and sell Castlemaine in the UK, currently held by Carlsberg-Tetley from New Zealand group Lion Nathan, the original brand owner, could be open for review when it changes ownership
No-one at Bass was immediately available for comment
Castlemaine XXXX is an Australian lager and is currently the subject of a new advertising campaign seeking to move the product away from its macho male image
Under the terms of the weekend deal, Bass will buy Allied Domecq's 50 percent interest in Carlsberg-Tetley and beer brands for 200 million stg
