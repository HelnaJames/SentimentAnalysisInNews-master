German companyfailures up15 pct in 96-Hermes
HAMBURG 1996-08-20Hermes Kreditversicherungs-AG, Germany's biggest credit insurer, saiditexpects 25,000 company insolvenciesduring the current year
Theinsurersaid on Tuesday that this would represent a 15 percentriseinbankruptciescompared to the previous year
On the European levelthe outlookwasworseningas well
Hermessaid it expected asix percent increaseinbankruptciesin Europe this year
In 1995 the numberstagnated
The Hermes forecast came despite its expectations of an upswing in the European economy during the second half of 1996
Hermes said this was due to the momentum given to the economy by monetary policy, in particular by low interest rates
Hermes said it recorded an 11 percent increase in premium income to 438 million marks ($294 million) during the first half of this year
At the same time the volume of claims increased
 Hermes said bankruptcies of big companies such as shipbuilder Bremer Vulkan Verbund and construction group Maculan were responsible for this development
