World oil pricesfall sharplyon profit-taking
LONDON 1996-08-23World oil pricestook ahammeringlate on Friday largely on extended profit-taking in the London futures market ahead of a long holiday weekend, traders said
London October futuresfor international benchmark Brent crude closed at $20
41 a barrel,down31 cents on the day
Earlier the contract traded close to four-month highs,propped up by rising demand for energyahead of the northern hemisphere winter
"It's extended profit-taking and weakness in the crude,"a floor brokersaid
A flood of North Sea physical cargoes which appeared on the market on Friday combined with economics which made it too expensive to ship crude oil across the Atlantic to the huge U
S
 market led to a sharp fall on the spot market
However Brent is averaging $2 a barrel more than 1995's $16
94, defying analysts' expectations that a surge in supplies would outweigh extra demand
Analysts cited stronger than expected demand growth for oil products this year from China, other developing Asian nations, Latin America and the U
S
"It's all down to demand," said one London-based energy market analyst
 "There are lot of hidden pockets of extra demand
Heating oil and diesel prices have underpinned oil market strength from Asia to the United States
U
S
 stocks of distillate fuel, which includes diesel and heating oil, were 18
5 million barrels below one year ago, according to the latest American Petroleum Institute report
Chinese oil products in the first seven months of the year soared 56 percent compared to the same period of 1995, official figures released this week showed
European oil traders said Brazil bought 300,000 tonnes of European gas oil in August, and was looking for more supplies in September
 This compares to a total of 700,000 tonnes imported over the whole of 1995, they added
Asian refiners have continued to plunder West African crude markets, buying another 500,000 barrels per day (bpd) for September on top of 500,000 bpd in August and 800,000 bpd in July
In previous years Asian purchases from Atlantic Basin producers have been limited to the period October-April
Crude markets on Friday also found support from a New York Times report that investigators had found evidence that TWA Flight 800 was downed by an explosive device
The paper said chemists at the Federal Bureau of Investigation had found traces of plastic explosives on a piece of wreckage retrieved from the jet, which crashed off New York's Long Island on July 17
Earlier this month the United States said Iran was the leading sponsor of terrorism against its interests
 Oil dealers are concerned that proof of Iranian involvement could provoke retaliation and destabilise the Middle East
