BBC call for more cash wins little sympathy.
A call from the BBC for Britons to pay more for its services received a frosty response on Saturday from politicans and media industry figures.
BBC Director-General John Birt said on Friday that the television licence fee should be increased above the level of inflation to protect what he called "the most successful cultural institution in the world".
Speaking at the Edinburgh International Television Festival, Birt said the first increase in real terms since 1985 was needed to help fund an expansion of services in the digital era.
But the ruling Conservative party and opposition Labour are reluctant to endorse a rise in the run-up to a general election due by next May.
