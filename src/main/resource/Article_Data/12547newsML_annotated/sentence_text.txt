Taiwan minister vows to bring 1996 GDP to 6
0 pct
TAIPEI 1996-08-24Taiwan's economics ministerWang Chih-kangsaid on Saturday he would do his best to bring the island's economic growth to 6
0 percent this year, despite a pessimistic forecast by the statistics bureau
The Directorate General of Budget, Accounting and Statistics on Friday revised its forecast for the 1996gross domestic productto 5
89 percent,well below an predictionof 6
17 percent issued three months ago
Wang was quoted by the state-funded Central News Agency as saying his ministry would try to revive the slow economy by promoting new investment projects totalling T$7
0 billion to bring the whole year economic growth to 6
0 percent
The economics ministry would take measures to boost local manufacturers's investment willingness and provide incentives for foreign investment, Wang was quoted as saying
The ministry has targetted the world's top 500 firms and would send missions to Europe, Japan and the U
S
 to promote investment, Wang said
 No further details were available
The island's GDP grew 5
42 percent in the second quarter compared to a revised 5
12 percent in the first quarter
The central bank cut banks' reserve requirements by up to 0
5 percentage point on Friday in a bid to revive the economy
