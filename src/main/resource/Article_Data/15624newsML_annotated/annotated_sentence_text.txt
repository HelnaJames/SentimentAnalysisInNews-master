At the New York Coffee Sugar and Cocoa Exchange on Monday, September coffee rose 4.20 cents per pound and closed at 136.45 cents, its highest close since September 6, 1995.
 But early trading on Tuesday saw a downward correction to around 134.00.
British markets were closed for a holiday on Monday and London futures for Robusta coffee had to play catch-up.
Coffee rallies, then pares the gains.

World coffee prices have been trading close around their highest for a year but Tuesday saw signs that the rally could be losing momentum.
 Torrential rain and gales in the world number two producer, Colombia, had aroused worries about possible damage to the crop.
Of more immediate concern to traders was a shortage of quality coffee available for delivery against the September Arabica grade futures contract in New York.
