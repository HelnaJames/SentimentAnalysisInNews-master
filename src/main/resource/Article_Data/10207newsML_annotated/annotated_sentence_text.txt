UPS to start five French feeder flights.
United Parcel Service Inc of America, (UPS) said it will launch five, weekday feeder flights in France from September 10.
"Two of the flights will operate between our French hub at Paris-Charles de Gaulle Airport and Nantes and Strasbourg while the other three will operate between Lyon-Satolas Airport and Bordeaux, Toulouse and Nice," UPS International Engineering manager for France, Owen Phillips, told Reuters.
Five two-tonne cargo capacity Beechcraft 1900 aircraft will operate on the new feeder routes, Phillips said
The aircraft are being leased from a French airline  whose identity UPS was not prepared to disclose, he added.
