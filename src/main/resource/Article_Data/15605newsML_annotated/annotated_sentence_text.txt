 Fator Securities said it was maintaining its buy recommendation on Brazilian food company Ceval.
 It said Ceval would begin to show signs of a turnaround in the third quarter of 1996 and reverse its losses by the end of the year.
 "Ceval has great growth potential and will obtain a better market position," it said in a report.
The company reported a 24 million real loss in the first half.
Ceval preferred shares were traded at 8.01 reais, up 0.12 percent, on the Sao Paulo bourse at 1207 local/1507 GMT.
