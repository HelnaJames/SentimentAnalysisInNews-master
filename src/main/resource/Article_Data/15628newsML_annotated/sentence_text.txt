United News to sell some local newspapers
LONDON 1996-08-27United News &amp; Mediasaid its regional newspaper division had decided to divest its newspaper holdings in Wales and the South East of England
United Newssaid thedecisionwaspromptedby the desire to focus on its primary areas in the north of England
"Theproposed divestmentwill allow us toconcentrate on and developour local media interestsin the North, where we already have a strong portfolio of titles," said United News chief executiveClive Hollick
United Provincial Newspapers (UPN), the UK regional newspaper division of United News, is the third largest regional publisher in the UK
It had operating profits of 28
6 million stg in 1995 on revenue of 184
4 million stg, the bulk of which was generated from its northern titles
Up for sale are UPN South East and UPN South Wales
UPN South East includes 28 separate free weekly newspapers in London and the surrounding area
 UPN South Wales has a portfolio of 19 free and paid-for titles including the daily South Wales Argus
UPN's main titles include the Yorkshire Post, Yorkshire Evening Post and the Sheffield Star plus the Lancashire Evening Post
 It also operates one of the north's leading newspaper printing centres at Broughton near Preston
United News recently merged with media and financial services group MAI Plc
The group publishes national newspapers The Daily Express, Sunday Express and Daily Star and operates the commercial ITV television franchises for southern and eastern England
The planned divestments are the latest in a series of sales of UK local newspaper groups
Earlier this month Pearson sold its Westminster Press local newspaper unit to Newsquest Media Group for 305 million stg
In June, EMAP sold its UK regional newspapers to Johnston Press for 211 million stg
