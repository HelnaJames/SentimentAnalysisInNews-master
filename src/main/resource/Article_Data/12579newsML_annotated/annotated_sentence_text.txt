About 350 athletes from nine countries set out on a 323-mile (517 km) climbing, rafting, biking and running endurance race thorugh the Canadian wilderness on Saturday.
The event, called the Eco-Challenge, is part of a growing sport known as adventure racing in which competitors test their limits for days over a perilous wilderness course.
The Eco-Challenge has been staged twice before -- in Utah and Maine last year -- and is modelled on similar races overseas.
The 70 teams in this year's race will will trek glaciers, climb mountains, whitewater raft, horseback ride, canoe and mountain bike on the gruelling course.
The location of this year's race was keep a secret until Friday evening.
